# Product Guidelines - Movie Ticket Booking System

## Tone and Voice
- **Professional yet Welcoming:** The system communicates with clarity and precision (especially during checkout), while maintaining a friendly, concierge-like approach to movie discovery.
- **Cinematic Flare:** Use evocative language that mirrors the excitement of the cinema experience, without sacrificing clarity.
- **Direct & Reassuring:** In critical moments like error handling or booking confirmations, the voice remains direct, explaining the situation clearly and providing actionable next steps to maintain user trust.

## Visual Identity
- **Dark & Thematic Aesthetic:** The user-facing interface should employ a high-contrast "Dark Mode" as the primary theme, reminiscent of a theater environment. This ensures that movie posters and vibrant artwork remain the visual focus.
- **Modern & Polished:** Use clean typography and consistent spacing to ensure the application feels modern and high-end.

## UI/UX Principles
- **Clarity First:** Booking flows and primary actions (like "Book Now" or "Select Seat") must be prominent and unambiguous. Minimize friction in the path to purchase.
- **Visual Richness:** Prioritize the display of high-quality movie assets. The interface should feel like a curated gallery of cinematic content.
- **Mobile-First Responsiveness:** Every feature must be fully functional and visually optimized for mobile devices, acknowledging that many users will book on the go.

## Admin Panel Standards
- **Utility-Focused Hybrid:** The admin interface should prioritize functionality and data density while maintaining a clean, modern aesthetic. Use intuitive dashboards and well-structured tables to allow for efficient management of movies and showtimes.
- **Efficiency Over Flare:** While consistent with the brand's polish, the admin panel should focus on reducing the time and effort required for administrative tasks.
